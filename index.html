Hi, I'm Meghan Thommes!

I'm currently a PhD Candidate at Boston University in the Department of Biomedical Engineering. I work with Daniel Segrè to investigate microbial communities.

I worked with Taiyao Wang, Qi Zhao, Yannis Paschalidis, and Daniel Segrè to develop a method (Division Of Labor in Microbial Networks) to predict how microbes share tasks (or don't) in communities. We applied a Mixed Integer Linear Program (MILP) to a metabolic model of Escherichia coli to create different strains of E. coli and predict their growth rates and metabolism. We limited the number of metabolic reactions each E. coli strain can perform, thereby reducing their metabolic capabilities, and observed that specialized strains in communities could survive with less individual metabolic reactions than isolated strains, catalogued which metabolites were exchanged, and identified several different specialization strategies.

I'm now formulating a MILP approach with Yannis Paschalidis and Daniel Segrè to predict how different yeast strains will grow in a variety of environments.
